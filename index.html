Sabrina Gonzalez Pasterski

At age 14 Sabrina had built her own aircraft and flown it solo!

Who Is Sabrina Gonzalez Pasterski?

She is an American theoretical physicist from Chicago who studies high energy physics. She was born on June 3, 1993. She completed her undergraduate studies at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), earned her PhD from Harvard University and is a PCTS Postdoctoral Fellow at Princeton University.

Some Astounding Facts About Her

  • She is known for her unusual list of accomplishments including a 5.00 undergraduate GPA from MIT.
  • She took her first flying lesson in 2003 and piloted FAA1 at EAA AirVenture Oshkosh in 2005.
  • In 2006, she started building a kit aircraft, and her first U.S. solo flight was in that kit aircraft in 2009.
  • Named to the Forbes 30 under 30 Science list in 2015 and named a Forbes 30 under 30 All Star in 2017. In 2018, she returned as a judge as part of Forbes' first ever all-female Science category judging panel.
  • Named the #3 Trending Scientist for all of 2017, according to Google Trends.
  • Her early work as a graduate student at Harvard resulted in the discovery of the "spin memory effect" which may be used to detect or verify the net effects of gravitational waves.
  • She earned her PhD in Physics from Harvard University in May 2019.
